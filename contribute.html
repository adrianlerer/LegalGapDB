‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be a lawyer to contribute?

No! While legal expertise is valuable, we also need researchers, developers, data scientists, and anyone with knowledge of how law works in practice in their jurisdiction.

How much time does contributing require?

A single case contribution typically takes 2-4 hours to research and document properly. You can contribute as little or as much as your schedule allows.

What if I find errors in existing cases?

Great! Please submit corrections via GitHub Issues or Pull Requests. Maintaining data quality is crucial for the project's credibility.

How is my contribution credited?

All contributors are credited in case metadata, on the contributors page, and in any resulting academic publications. Major contributors may be invited as co-authors on research papers.

What languages can I contribute in?

Cases can include content in local languages, but we require English abstracts for all cases to ensure global accessibility. We're working on expanding multilingual support.
